We strongly encourage all our players to make use of our player protection tools. You can set daily, weekly, or monthly limits on the amount of money you can deposit into your account. Loss Limits:

You can set a limit on the maximum net amount you are willing to lose over a daily, weekly, or monthly period. The system will prevent you from continuing to play once this limit has been reached, helping to protect you from chasing losses.

Wager Limits:

Identifying a Potential Problem

Responsible gambling means knowing when to stop. If you are concerned about your gambling habits or the habits of someone you know, ask yourself the following questions honestly:

  • Do you spend more money or time gambling than you can afford or intend to?
  • Have you ever lied about the extent of your gambling or hidden it from friends and family?
  • Are you chasing your losses, hoping to win back money you have lost?
  • Has gambling negatively impacted your work, studies, or personal relationships?
  • Do you gamble as a way to escape from stress, anxiety, or other problems?
  • Have you ever sold possessions or borrowed money to fund your gambling?
  • Do you feel a sense of guilt or regret after gambling?
  • Do you find it difficult to stop or control your gambling?

If you answered "yes" to one or more of these questions, it may be a sign of a gambling problem, and we strongly encourage you to seek help and make use of our self-exclusion tools immediately.

Seeking Help and Support

Recognizing that you may have a problem is the first and most important step. There are many independent, non-profit organizations dedicated to providing free and confidential support to anyone affected by problem gambling. We highly recommend contacting one of them:

Gamblers Anonymous:

A fellowship of individuals who share their experience, strength, and hope to solve their common problem and help others to recover from a gambling problem.
Website: www.gamblersanonymous.org

GamCare:

A leading UK-based provider of information, advice, and support for the prevention and treatment of problem gambling. They offer a confidential helpline and online support.
Website: www.gamcare.org.uk

Gambling Therapy:

A global online support service that offers multilingual advice and support to people affected by problem gambling.
Website: www.gamblingtherapy.org

ConnexOntario (For Canadian Players):

Provides free and confidential health services information for people experiencing problems with alcohol and drugs, mental illness, or gambling.
Website: www.connexontario.ca
